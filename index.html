About Me

I'm Akanji Anthony Abidemi, a 400L Anatomy student at the University of Ilorin with a parallel passion for frontend development. My journey in tech began with a curiosity for building interactive web experiences, which evolved into expertise in React.js and modern frontend development. I thrive on solving problems at the intersection of medicine and technology, believing that bridging these fields can revolutionize healthcare solutions.

As a developer, I specialize in crafting responsive, user-friendly interfaces with React, and I'm continuously expanding my skills—currently eyeing mastery of the MERN stack and Next.js for full-stack capabilities. In my medical studies, I focus on anatomical research and data-driven approaches, always looking for ways to integrate tech for better diagnostics or medical education tools.

Beyond academics and coding, I enjoy contributing to open-source projects, writing tech articles, and exploring AI's role in healthcare. These pursuits keep me innovative and connected to both fields. My long-term vision is to develop software solutions that address gaps in medical education or patient care, leveraging my dual expertise.

Fun fact: I approach coding like anatomy—breaking down complex systems into smaller, logical components to understand (and build) them better. Whether it’s a UI component or a physiological process, the joy lies in mastering the details.
